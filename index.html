    About Me

    Coding and software development have become one of the most intelligent forms of craft, and I reckon, the most creative. There's often a misconception that creativity is solely linked to artistry.


    My solid background in marketing campaigns, working with a full variety of creative assets, has led me to the strong conclusion that inventing new tools, optimizing existing ones, and ensuring their security are all profound forms of creativity that are driving the future.


    I love ads—whether video, email, or banners—as prime examples of optimized, well-thought-out art. They must convey a very to-the-point, concise message with a specific purpose: 'tell and sell.' The stakes are even higher when a product is niche, or when its underlying concept is innovative and complex. This excites me a lot as it requires even more marketing ingenuity and vision to succeed.


    Complex technologies desperately need a good, clear narrative to reach potential customers. This is what interests me, and exactly what I want to work with!

    I can tell that story, and I can position it effectively.


    Drive toward results, even when everyone else is having fun.

    EventyOn - culinary event studio.

    I was 24 when my teenage dream came true: I got to work with former MTV and comedy club production. I was recruited by the CEO of a media agency for impressive communication skills. These I developed to a high degree running corporate culinary events to an audience sometimes of 150+ pax. Brought together in a competing teams, guided by Italian chefs, they enjoyed aperitif and friendly rivalry. This quickly taught me how to organize and motivate people of diverse occupations and ranks, while ensuring the results of their mutual efforts were on top of expectations. These skills proved invaluable in the media industry.

    Proplacement, sponsorship media agency.

    The dynamic and fast-paced nature of media sponsorship was striking. Product placement, digital campaigns, social media, shoots, and production – everything had to be completed within the shortest possible timeframe. To hit the target, campaigns required close attention to metrics and analytics to ensure that every bit of budget was allocated correctly. To keep everything on track, I turned to frameworks, most of which originated from the tech industry.

    To be credible, learn to speak their language.

    Gazprom Media Holding.

    It was the year of record profits from sponsorship campaigns when I joined Media Holding. The job was more intense, more responsible, and even more interesting because of it. Three precious lessons I’ve learned from my time there:

    1. Ask the right questions to get things done right and fast when working with several functional teams.
    2. To ask the right question: learn to speak their language by learning as much as possible about each process of every functional team.
    3. Digital campaigns are more versatile and effective, as they allow for interactions and more accurate analytics.

    If wishes were fishes, we would cast nets.
    So I learned how to code instead.
